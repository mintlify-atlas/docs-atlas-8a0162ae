The onboarding process helps Echo understand your work style and preferences, enabling more relevant and helpful proactive assistance.

## Onboarding Overview

After signing in for the first time, you'll answer a series of questions to customize Echo's behavior. This takes approximately 2-3 minutes to complete.

<Info>
All onboarding preferences can be changed later in the Settings screen.
</Info>

## Step 1: Tell Us About Yourself

Echo asks two questions to understand your work context:

### What Best Describes Your Day?

Choose the persona that best matches your role:

- **Developer**: For engineers and technical professionals
- **Founder / Manager**: For business leaders and team managers
- **Designer / Creative**: For creative professionals and designers
- **Student / Research**: For students and researchers
- **Sales / Ops**: For sales and operations roles
- **Personal**: For personal productivity and life management

<Note>
Your persona selection helps Echo recommend relevant toolkit integrations and prioritize the right types of actions.
</Note>

### Where Does Most of Your Work Live?

Select your primary workspace:

- **Inbox**: Email-centric workflow (Gmail, Outlook)
- **Calendar**: Meeting and schedule-driven work
- **Docs / Sheets**: Document and spreadsheet collaboration
- **Tasks**: Task management systems (Linear, TickTick)
- **Code**: Development environments and repositories

This helps Echo understand where to focus its proactive assistance and which integrations to prioritize.

## Step 2: Confirmation Preference

Configure how Echo handles action execution:

### Ask First (Recommended)
- Echo proposes actions and waits for your approval
- You review each action before it executes
- Provides maximum control and transparency
- Best for getting started with Echo

### Auto-Execute
- Echo executes approved action types automatically
- Reduces friction for routine tasks
- You receive notifications after execution
- Recommended after you're comfortable with Echo's behavior

<Warning>
Auto-execute mode should only be enabled after you're familiar with Echo's action patterns and trust its decision-making.
</Warning>

## Step 3: Notification Preferences

Choose how you want to be notified about Echo's activities:

### All Notifications
- Receive real-time alerts for every action
- Immediate awareness of all Echo activity
- Best for users who want maximum visibility

### Urgent Only
- Notifications only for time-sensitive or high-priority actions
- Reduces notification volume while staying informed
- Balanced approach for most users

### Daily Digest
- Single summary notification once per day
- Review all actions in a consolidated view
- Minimal interruption to your workflow

### Silent
- No push notifications
- Check Echo manually when convenient
- For users who prefer pull-based updates

<Tip>
Start with "Urgent Only" to find the right balance between awareness and notification fatigue.
</Tip>

## Step 4: Recommended Integrations

Based on your persona and work location, Echo suggests relevant toolkit integrations:

### For Developers
- **Linear**: Issue and project tracking
- **GitHub**: Code repository management
- **Slack**: Team communication

### For Founders/Managers
- **Slack**: Team collaboration
- **Linear**: Project management
- **Calendar**: Schedule optimization

### For Personal Use
- **TickTick**: Task management
- **Calendar**: Time blocking
- **Notes**: Capture and organize thoughts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Recommendations">
    See which toolkits Echo suggests based on your answers.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect or Skip">
    Tap any toolkit to start the connection flow, or skip to complete onboarding.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Up Later">
    You can always connect toolkits later from the Toolkits tab.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Skipping toolkit setup during onboarding doesn't limit Echo's functionality. You can add integrations anytime.
</Info>

## Completing Onboarding

After answering all questions:

1. Your preferences are saved to your profile
2. Echo navigates to the Home tab
3. You can immediately start using the app
4. Push notification permissions are requested (if not already granted)

## Modifying Preferences Later

All onboarding preferences can be updated after initial setup:

1. Navigate to the **Settings** tab (bottom navigation)
2. Scroll to the **Preferences** section
3. Tap any preference to modify:
   - Persona
   - Work Location
   - Automation (Ask First vs Auto-Execute)
   - Notifications

<Note>
Changing your persona or work location will update Echo's toolkit recommendations and action prioritization.
</Note>

## Push Notification Setup

After onboarding completes, Echo requests permission for push notifications:

### iOS
- Tap "Allow" when the system prompt appears
- If denied, enable in Settings > Echo > Notifications

### Android
- Tap "Allow" when prompted
- If denied, enable in Settings > Apps > Echo > Notifications

<Tip>
Even if you selected "Silent" notification preference, enabling push notifications allows Echo to send urgent alerts when needed.
</Tip>

## What Happens Next

After onboarding:
- Echo begins monitoring your connected tools
- You can add thoughts by tapping the "+" button
- Actions appear in the Home tab as Echo identifies opportunities
- Review and approve pending actions

Continue to [Managing Actions](/mobile/managing-actions) to learn how to work with Echo's proactive suggestions.
